## Final answer

Google completed (closed) its acquisition of cloud security company **Wiz** on **March 11, 2026**, for an all-cash purchase price of **$32 billion** [1][2].

Here are the key details of the deal:

- **Announcement date:** March 18, 2025, when Google signed a definitive agreement to acquire Wiz [2].
- **Closing date:** March 11, 2026 [1].
- **Price:** $32 billion, all-cash (subject to customary closing adjustments) [1][2].
- **Significance:** This is the **largest acquisition in Google's history** [1].
- **Regulatory path:** U.S. regulatory approval was received in November 2025, and EU regulatory approval followed in February 2026 [1].
- **Background:** Google had previously made an initial offer of ~$23 billion in 2024, which Wiz CEO Assaf Rappaport rejected. The companies returned to negotiations and agreed on the $32 billion figure in early 2025 [1].

Once closed, Wiz — a leading multicloud security platform headquartered in New York — joined Google Cloud, with its products continuing to work across competing cloud platforms including AWS, Microsoft Azure, and Oracle Cloud [2].
